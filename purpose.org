* Discovering Purpose

#+FILETAGS: purpose

** Why it matters

Philipians 3:7-14

- Paul's purpose transcends doing
- Purpose is not something you're supposed to do, but something you're
  supposed to be
  - Doing flows from being
- God's intention is that each person fulfills their highest potential
  in Christ
- Purpose provides:
  - a destination
  - meaning for our past experiences (including achievements,
    mistakes)
  - filter for life decisions and priorities
  - motivation amid setbacks
- Jeremiah 1:5 - God is not random, everything is by divine intention

** What it is not

Luke 12:22-34

- a list of things to have, do or achieve
- what you should be doing, but who you should be becoming
- it is not a decision, but a discovery (2 samuel 5:12)
- it is not a future far-off event, but it is now
- It is not dependant on time, money or connections, they may help or
  hinder
- It is not destiny, we have to actively collaborate with God

** Principles of purpose

Romans 8:27-30

- 2 Timothy 1:9: Divinely inspired
- Isaiah 14:27: Universally applied to all and to each
  - Ephesians 1:9-11
    - God's purpose (the why) informs his will (what, how, when)
    - His purpose is Christ-centered - summing up all things in Him
      - We are predestined according to this purpose
	- Romans 8:28,29: to be conformed to the image of his son
- Ephesians 4:4-7: Individually interpreted
  - Each one has their own unique DNA, fingerprint, sets of
    experiences, skills etc
- Progressively revealed

** Being conformed to the image of his son

Hebrews 1:1-4, John 14:9, John 10:30

- The Son is the exact representation of His being

Jesus revealed that he is:

1) Life :: (1 john 5:11) - salvation, healing, redemption
2) Light :: (John 8:12, 1 John 1:5) - Wisdom, truth, discernment, justice
3) Love (Ahava) :: (John 15:9, 1 John 4:8) - Mercy, Grace, forgiveness

1 John 2:5, 6, 4:12-17

- Our ultimate destination is to be perfected in him.

- Based on the principle of light, life, and love, we turn these into verbs:
  - life -> live
  - light -> learn
  - love -> love
- Our purpose is to live, to learn and to love
  - Live everyday for God's glory
    - physical and spiritual life
  - Learn the lessons we need to learn
    - growth in grace and knowledge of Christ, spiritual wisdom and maturity
  - Love God, be loved by him and share this love with others
    - being perfected in love

- 1 Timothy 2:1-6: The progression:
  - Life as the gate (all to be saved)
  - Light as the path (knowledge of truth)
  - Perfection in love as the destination (conformed to the image of Christ)

John 8:1-12
- Life, light and love are expressions of the Christ in a broken
  world of human weakness
    
*** To Live

John 10:9-11

- The enemy comes to steal, kill and destroy
- Jesus came so that we may have abundant life
  - The very life of the eternal God that cannot be stolen, killed or
    destroyed
- Ephesians 2:4-6, John 14:6, 1 John 5:12, Galatians 2:20, Colossians 3:17
  - The Christ gave his life, so that we could receive the
    indestructible life of God
  - Affirmations:
    - We live today for the glory of God being grateful for all things
    - We live today by his power and not our own, Christ will strengthen us

*** To learn

1 John 1:5-7

- The thief operates in darkness - fear, confusion, deception
- Light is the difficult path:
  - The easy path requires nothing and teaches nothing
  - The difficult path requires everything and teaches reality/exposes
    non-reality
  - The difficulties are our internal drama - fear, doubt, worries,
    ego etc
  - We overcome our internal drama by cooperating with God
- John 8:12, John 12:35, Proverbs 4:18,19, Ephesians 5:8, Colossians
  2:6,7, 2 peter 3:18
  - Affirmations
    - We will be humble enough today to learn the lessons we need in
      order to acquire wisdom and maturity
    - Today we will walk in the light as the Christ is in the light,
      learning what the light reveals
    - Today we will grown in knowledge and grace of the Lord Jesus one
      step at a time

*** To Love (Ahava)

Matthew 5:43-48

- Light and Life without love is nothing:
  - Life is the power and strength of love
  - Light is the wisdom and maturity of love

- Difference from new age philosophy
  - It rightly wants love without religion
  - However love without light has no wisdom, and love without life
    has no power
  - Light and Life are found in the Christ

- John 3:16-17, Romans 5:6-10, 1 John 4:16-18
  - Being perfected in love is to be complete, lacking nothing
  - Affirmations:
    - We will today love God, and be loved by him
    - We will today freely receive all that God has given, and freely
      give what we have received
    - We will today remember that when all else fails, love never
      fails

*** In Summary

- To live everyday for the glory of God
- To learn the lessons we need to learn
- To love God, be loved by him, and freely share this love with others

By doing these, it is impossible to make the wrong moves and miss our
predestined purpose.

** The 4 big questions

1) Who am I?
2) Where did I come from?
3) Why am I here?
4) Where am I going?

All the major world religions, belief systems and philosophies attempt
to answer this questions.

John 8:31-36, 42-47

- Jesus, being perfectly God and perfectly man, knew who he was, where
  he came from, why he was here and where he was going.
  
  - "Who am I?" The son of God
  - "Where did I come from?" I came from the Father
  - "Why am I here?" To be life, light and show the love of God
  - "Where am I going?" I am going back to the Father
    
Matthew 10:39

- Losing your life (psyche, soul, self) is finding it. We cannot
  truely know ourselves unless we know the Lord

*** The 4 big answers

Looking at the Lord:

 - Who am I? I am a child of God
 - Where did I come from? I was created and sent (predestined) by God
 - Why am I here? To live, to learn and to love (each day at a time)
 - Where am I going? I will return to God, perfected in love (ahava)
   and be with him forever

It applies to everyone, including non-believers:

 - Who are you? A prodigal child of God
 - Where did you come from? Created and sent by God
 - Why are you here? To live, learn and love
 - Where are you going? Believe in the Christ, be perfected in love
   and return to God

** You are the temple

1 Corinthians 3:9-17

- The world measures success, purpose provides significance.
- Acts 7:47-50, 15:24-26: The body replaced the physical temple
- The old temple had:
  - show bread
  - a lampstand
  - The ark
- The new temple has:
  - life: mirrors the bread for provision
  - light: mirrors the lampstand for a guiding light - the path
  - love: mirrors the ark, God's presence

** Praying with purpose

Matthew 6:5-13

- God already knows our needs, so prayer is not primarily about asking
  for things.

- Elements of prayer:
  - our daily bread: mirrors the table of showbread - life
  - forgiveness of debts: mirrors the Golden lampstand - light
  - deliverance from evil: mirror the ark of the covenant (God's
    presence) - love

- Provision: John 6:5
- Path: John 8:12
- Presence: Romans 8:38,39

** Love overcomes all

Psalms 91

- If we have no love (sense that God is with us) then we are wrapped
  up
in fear and doubt
- Without light, life and love overflowing we cannot achieve a bigger
  thing.
  - Churchianity is going through the motions without life, light and
    love
- A focus on life, light and love day by day is fulfilling our purpose
  daily - we become a temple, God's dwelling place
- As we grow in the essentials, the rest - gifts, callings, missions
  will be progressively revealed.

** Purpose in Retrospect

Gen 45:1-8, 50:20

- Joseph could not recognize God's purpose while living it, only in
  retrospect.
  - He could not control what happened to him, only how to respond.
- Despite his imperfections (pride), he's an example of chosing to
  live, learn and love - forgiving his brothers can suggest perfection
  in love
  - Not blaming God and others - stewing in bitterness
  - Being in a good headspace allowed him to thrive by help others
    e.g. interpret crucial dreams
- The power of focus; we can choose what to focus on:
  - what we don't have vs what we have
  - Everthing wrong vs everthing right
  - The evils in the world, vs the goodness of God
    - News and social media spam us with all that is going wrong
  - What we can get vs what we can give
  - Things we can control vs things we cannot control
- Philipians 4:6-8: Changing your focus changes your feelings

** Purpose in the light of Roles and Responsibilities

Colossians 3:12-25

- Practical wisdom for living
- Life, light and love expressed in the daily, ordinary things
- In each relationship we have roles and responsibilities to play
  - In life, we don't get to choose some of our relationships
  - We have roles to play that require wisdom and maturity (light)
  - Our responsibilities are founded on love - when all else fails,
    love never fails

** The Lord of the work

Acts 13:1-3

- There is plenty to do daily as we live, learn and love in the light
  of our roles and responsibilities
- If we are called for something more, we minister to the Lord first,
  then he will send us forth at the right time
  - We need to first be faithful in the basics of everyday living
  - The work of the Lord is not more important than the Lord of the
    work: Ephesians 2:10, 5:8, Colossians 1:10-13
